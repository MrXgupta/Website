Action Games

Subgenres: Shooter, Fighting, Platformer, Beat 'em up

Description: Focuses on physical challenges, including hand-eye coordination and reaction time. Examples include "Call of Duty," "Street Fighter," and "Super Mario."

Adventure Games

Subgenres: Text-based, Graphic Adventure, Visual Novel

Description: Emphasizes storytelling and exploration, often involving puzzle-solving and character interaction. Notable games include "The Legend of Zelda" and "Monkey Island."

Role Playing Game

Subgenres: Action RPG, Turn-Based RPG, Tactical RPG, MMORPG

Description: Players assume the roles of characters in a fictional setting, focusing on story, character development, and strategy. Popular RPGs include "Final Fantasy," "The Witcher," and "World of Warcraft."

Simulation Games

Subgenres: Life Simulation, Vehicle Simulation, Construction and Management

Description: Simulates real-world activities or environments, often with a focus on realism. Examples include "The Sims," "Microsoft Flight Simulator," and "SimCity."

Strategy Games

Subgenres: Real-Time Strategy (RTS), Turn-Based Strategy (TBS), Tower Defense, 4X

Description: Requires players to plan and make decisions to achieve a goal, often involving resource management and tactical combat. Games like "StarCraft," "Civilization," and "Age of Empires" are prime examples.

Sports Games

Subgenres: Traditional Sports, Racing, Extreme Sports

Description: Simulates the practice of sports, including team sports, racing, and extreme activities. Notable titles include "FIFA," "NBA 2K," and "Gran Turismo."

Puzzle Games

Subgenres: Logic Puzzles, Match-Three, Physics-Based

Description: Focuses on problem-solving and logic, often requiring players to think critically and strategically. Popular puzzle games include "Tetris," "Candy Crush," and "Portal."

Horror Games

Subgenres: Survival Horror, Psychological Horror, Jump Scare

Description: Designed to invoke fear and suspense, often with eerie atmospheres and unsettling themes. Famous horror games include "Resident Evil," "Silent Hill," and "Outlast."

Fighting Games

Subgenres: 2D Fighting, 3D Fighting, Platform Fighters

Description: Fighting games focus on close combat between characters, usually in one-on-one matches. Players must utilize precise timing, strategy, and quick reflexes to defeat opponents by depleting their health bar. The genre often includes special moves, combos, and unique abilities for each character, creating a deep and competitive gameplay experience.

Racing Games

Subgenres: Arcade Racing, Simulation Racing, Kart Racing, Off-Road Racing

Description: Racing games simulate the experience of driving or racing vehicles, ranging from highly realistic simulations to more casual, arcade-style games. They often feature various vehicles, tracks, and racing modes, catering to different preferences and skill levels. Whether focusing on speed, technical driving, or creative tracks, racing games appeal to a broad audience.
